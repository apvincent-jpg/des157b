The First 8 Seconds

Roberto, Thaís. “The First 8 Seconds – Capturing the Attention of Gen Z Students.” KEG, 21 Aug. 2023, https://www.keg.com/news the-first-8-seconds-capturing-the-attention-of-gen-z-students

The first 8 seconds discusses the decrease in attention span of Gen Z individuals, from 12 seconds for millennials to 8 seconds. The study attributes this decrease to the constant exposure to multiple screens and so much digital content. This article continues to discuss ways marketers can capture the attention of Gen Z. This is by giving them immediate answers, creating video content, and keeping videos short and bold, but not overproduced. This article will help me develop effectively distracting notifications and ads to try and capture the user’s attention.

The Future of Education

“The Future of Education.” YouTube, uploaded by Bernard Marr, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UrvFotxTxF4.

The Future of Education discusses what learning might look like by 2035. Immersive virtual learning environments might exist. Students might be able to interact with tutors from around the world as though they were right next to them, through VR. These students could be conducting complex scientific experiments or exploring ancient civilizations from the comfort of a classroom or their home. Personalization is the future of education, where students get a tailored learning experience. AI mentors will guide academic learning, and will offer psychological support. This video brings up questions about what teachers will do and privacy.

The Negative Effects of Remove Learning on Children’s Well-Being

Parker, Kim. “The Negative Effects of Remote Learning on Children’s Well-Being.” Harvard Graduate School of Education, 2 Feb. 2022,https://www.gse.harvard.edu/ideas/edcast/22/02/negative-effects-remote-learning-childrens-well-being.

The Negative Effects of Remove Learning on Children’s Well-Being discusses the results of a study that began just before COVID hit, and examined the effects of the shift to online learning among families. Families reported a rise in temper tantrums, anxiety, and poor ability to manage emotions, especially among elementary-aged children doing remote learning. The study also stresses the importance of supporting children though transitions like moving from in person learning to online learning, as it can be especially stressful for younger kids.
