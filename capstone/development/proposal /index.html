Capstone Proposal

Topic

I want to explore the topic of what the future of education (specifically elementary to middle school age) looks like given the rise of AI use in classrooms, and the growing number of teachers who are quitting due to the kids' behavior.

Project Idea

I will be using a dystopian lens while developing this project, which will aim to show users a scary but possible future for education. I want to create a kind of interface seemingly similar to canvas or an online learning platform, however with drastically different features. In this imagined future, AI is the teacher, and kids spend the entire day learning on computers. The application would monitor everything about the child, such as how often they are day dreaming (if a neuro monitor is connected to the device), how fast they are able to read, and even having a section that predicts future career and lifestyle paths for the child. The child’s classmates would all be avatars on one section of the screen. Moreover, advertisements could pop up every now and then if kids do not have the free version of this application. Regarding what libraries I will be using, I need to explore more. I imagine I will need to use something that helps randomize features like pop up ads and daydreaming/focus notifications.

Possible Challenges

It might be challenging to make this idea come across strongly, and to really make people feel like this type of education is very different from what we have now, since education apps similar to this already exist. I want to stress the inescapability of it, and I think the ads, and neuro monitor notifications will help with that. Although, I also worry that the neuro monitor is its own separate thing that could require too much explanation. I hope it’s just something I can slip in there as though it is just a normal part of this imagined future.

Audience

I would say my audience is parents, specifically expecting parents or parents of young children. I want to stress the importance of the education that happens outside of fact learning. Being around other kids, reading physical books, playing in nature, and experimenting with music and art are just as important for child development. Furthermore, in a world where children are becoming increasingly competitive, as college admissions and job opportunities grow more competitive as well, it is important for parents not to smother or label their children, but instead allow them to enjoy their childhood.
